## Key learnings (the honest ones)
- mAP 0.904 is inflated — measured against self-labels, not ground truth
- Real recall ~20-30% in dense uniform canopy (crown merging problem, not model problem)
- Precision ~80-90% on what IS detected (based on visual review)
- NDVI can't distinguish trees from bushes (both chlorophyll-rich)
- LiDAR height is the ONLY reliable tree-vs-bush discriminator
- Foundation segmentation models (SAM2) help modestly on distinct crowns, not on uniform canopy
- Detection and classification should be separate concerns (post-hoc classifier architecture)

## Core architectural principle

**Detect once, classify many times.**
RF-DETR finds crown-like regions. A separate classifier decides what each one is.
The detector stays frozen unless evidence says otherwise. Classification improvements
iterate in seconds (retrain XGBoost) instead of 20+ minutes (retrain RF-DETR).
